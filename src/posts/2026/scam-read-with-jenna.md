I received an email recently suggesting that Jenna Bush Hager was suddenly interested in spotlighting my [*Learning Progressive Web Apps*](https://www.amazon.com/Learning-Progressive-Apps-John-Wargo/dp/0136484220){target="_blank"}:

```text
Hi John,

I’m Jenna, founder of Read with Jenna, a book club and reading community dedicated to spotlighting insightful and impactful books across different genres and industries.

I recently came across Learning Progressive Web Apps: Building Modern Web Apps Using Service Workers, and I was especially drawn to the practical and forward-thinking approach the book takes toward modern web development. The focus on Progressive Web Apps and service workers makes it highly relevant in today’s digital landscape, especially for developers looking to create faster, more reliable, and engaging web experiences. It’s the kind of educational and innovative content our community would find both valuable and inspiring.

That’s why I’m reaching out. We would love to feature your book as part of an upcoming Read with Jenna selection. Our readers enjoy discovering books that combine technical expertise with real-world application, and your work feels like an excellent fit for meaningful discussion and engagement.

We’re currently finalizing our next lineup and would be excited to include your book. Would you be open to collaborating with us on this feature?

Kind regards,

photo
	
Jenna Bush Hager
Book Club Specialist, Read With Jenna

jennabushhagerbookclub@gmail.com  |  www.bushcenter.org

facebook | instagram | tiktok
```

I didn't believe it for a second, mostly because I can't imagine Jenna Bush wanting technical books in her program. I did some research and it seems that my original assessment was correct. Forward thinking? A forward thinking web development book? Innovative content? Yeah, no.

Anyway, I got a follow-up email:

```text
Hi John,

I hope you’re doing well. I wanted to follow up on my previous message regarding Learning Progressive Web Apps: Building Modern Web Apps Using Service Workers and our interest in featuring it as part of an upcoming Read with Jenna selection.

The more we explored the book, the more we appreciated its practical and forward-thinking approach to modern web development. The focus on Progressive Web Apps and service workers, along with its emphasis on building faster, more reliable, and engaging web experiences, makes it especially relevant in today’s digital landscape.

We truly believe this book would resonate strongly with our community, particularly readers who enjoy technical learning paired with real-world application. It’s exactly the kind of insightful and practical content that encourages both learning and meaningful discussion.

We’d love the opportunity to collaborate with you and highlight Learning Progressive Web Apps in our upcoming lineup. If you’re interested, I’d be happy to share more details about what the feature could look like.
```

This basically repeats the content from the original email. The more we explored the book?

Anyway, I'm not going to respond; its still clearly a scam. A six year old tech book that nobody bought? Not likely to catch anyone's attention.
